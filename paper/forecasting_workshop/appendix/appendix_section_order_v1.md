# Appendix Section Order v1

## Recommended Order

1. **Appendix A. Dense-Friction Sensitivity**
   - Start with dense-friction because it is the strongest appendix-only sensitivity check for the main implementation-side story.
   - Keep the framing diagnostic and subordinate to the selected-point RL result.

2. **Appendix B. CC-TA-LBIP Auxiliary Evidence**
   - Follow with the auxiliary comparator to show same-direction support under the same-state, fixed-forecast-map setup.
   - Keep the auxiliary label explicit.

3. **Appendix C. Narrow `c`-Ablation Robustness**
   - Place the local `c`-ablation immediately after the comparator because it is only a robustness add-on for that auxiliary package.

4. **Appendix D. Target-versus-Executed Evaluation Disagreement**
   - Add the compact support table here so the evaluation-object disagreement is documented outside the main 4-page narrative.
   - Keep the framing diagnostic and tightly tied to the main accounting argument.

5. **Appendix E. Similar Forecasting Information, Different Realized Decision Quality**
   - Keep the same-forecast package in the appendix so forecasting relevance remains visible without making it look like a second main result.
   - State explicitly that this is metric-level support, not proof of exact forecast identity.

6. **Appendix F. Provenance Note**
   - Keep provenance after the scientific support sections.
   - Make it short, factual, and limited to wording integrity.

7. **Appendix G. Audit and Guardrail Summary**
   - End with the audit summary and guardrails so the appendix closes on scope control rather than on new empirical material.

## Section Roles

- Appendix A supports the friction-sensitive interpretation.
- Appendix B supports the same-direction implementation story in an auxiliary comparator.
- Appendix C shows local auxiliary robustness only.
- Appendix D records the target-versus-executed interpretation mismatch directly.
- Appendix E keeps the same-forecast package as conservative appendix support only.
- Appendix F records provenance facts only.
- Appendix G preserves submission guardrails only.

## Ordering Guardrails

- Do not let the appendix begin with provenance.
- Do not let the comparator appear before dense-friction sensitivity.
- Do not let the audit summary read like new evidence.
- Do not let any appendix section read like a second main result.

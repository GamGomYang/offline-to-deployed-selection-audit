# Results

The first results question is whether a forecasting-driven system should be judged on proposed targets or on realized actions once frictions intervene. Table 1 answers that question in the paper's concrete portfolio case study. With predictive information held fixed, the validation-selected interior point `eta=0.5` improves held-out executed-path net Sharpe relative to the immediate-execution baseline `eta=1.0` by `+0.0105` at `kappa=5e-4` and `+0.0213` at `kappa=1e-3`, while reducing median executed turnover from `0.02200` to `0.01095`. The `kappa=0` row remains near-flat at `-0.0002`. This makes the selected-point comparison the only main empirical result in the workshop paper and supports an implementation-side reading rather than a stronger-predictor reading.

Figure 1 explains why executed-path evaluation is primary. At `eta=0.5`, target turnover is about twice realized executed turnover across the locked cost rows (`TOtgt/TOexec ≈ 2.00`), tracking remains small but nonzero at about `0.00259`, and the final path gap grows with cost from `0.00067` at `kappa=0` to `0.00739` at `kappa=1e-3`. Target-level quantities are therefore diagnostic only: once frictions intervene between proposed targets and realized positions, the system should be judged on the realized path it actually induces. In other words, the choice of evaluation object is not merely presentational: in the positive-cost rows, target-based evaluation weakens or erases the selected-point improvement that remains visible on the realized executed path.

The point is not that smoothing is intrinsically beneficial; rather, once frictions separate desired targets from realized actions, the forecast-to-execution interface becomes part of the evaluated system, and its effects must be assessed on the realized path.

A conservative appendix-only support analysis, titled `Similar forecasting information, different realized decision quality`, shows that forecast-side metrics move only slightly while decision-side metrics move much more strongly in the positive-cost rows. With the fitted ridge forecast map held fixed, forecast MSE changes by only `-0.03%` at `kappa=5e-4` and `+0.28%` at `kappa=1e-3`, while executed turnover and realized cost fall by `90.8%` and `95.1%`, respectively. This supports forecasting relevance at the metric level without implying exact forecast identity.

Dense-friction and CC-TA-LBIP should be cited only as appendix-style support. The dense-friction figure is supplementary sensitivity evidence under the documented selected-point setup, and the comparator remains auxiliary supporting evidence rather than a second main result.

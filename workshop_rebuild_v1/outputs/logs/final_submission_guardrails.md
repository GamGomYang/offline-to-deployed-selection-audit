# Final Submission Guardrails

These guardrails define how `workshop_rebuild_v1` may be used as the final workshop submission bundle.

## Claim Guardrails

- Keep the primary claim exactly at the locked narrow level:
  - *In cost-sensitive portfolio decision systems, holding the predictive signal fixed, the forecast-to-execution interface materially changes realized decision quality.*
- Do not upgrade the paper into a stronger-predictor, superior-alpha, new-RL-method, benchmark-dominance, or general forecasting-systems claim.

## Evidence-Hierarchy Guardrails

- Keep the RL frozen-policy selected-point result central.
- Keep target-vs-executed accounting and dense-friction as supporting diagnostics for the same implementation-side story.
- Keep CC-TA-LBIP auxiliary.
- Keep the same-forecast package as supporting analysis only.

## Metric Guardrails

- Keep executed-path net Sharpe as the primary metric.
- Keep executed turnover and realized cost as supporting metrics.
- Keep target-path quantities diagnostic only.
- Do not promote target-path return, target turnover, or tracking discrepancy into headline evidence.

## Dense-Friction Guardrails

- Describe dense friction only as a **regenerated canonical diagnostic bundle under the locked protocol**.
- Do not call it source-artifact reproduction.
- Do not promote the best-interior line into the main result.
- Do not imply new adaptive eta logic.

## CC-TA-LBIP Guardrails

- Keep CC-TA-LBIP auxiliary in all wording.
- Do not describe it as a second identification result.
- If the narrow `c`-ablation is mentioned, describe it only as a local reviewer-facing robustness check.
- Do not convert the `c`-ablation into post-hoc retuning or proof of a uniquely optimal `c`.

## Same-Forecast Guardrails

- Keep the title at the `Similar forecasting information` level.
- Do not say `same forecast` or `exactly identical forecast` unless direct proof is added in a future audited step.
- Keep the note that previous executed weights remain part of the state.

## Bundle-Use Guardrails

- Use the `v2` writing files as the canonical submission-facing text set.
- Treat `v1` writing files and run logs as retained history only.
- Do not rely on the old lane for essential submission artifacts.
- If any future edit violates one of these guardrails, rerun an audit before treating the bundle as final.

The central empirical result is the frozen-policy selected-point comparison in Table 1. Changing only the forecast-to-execution interface changes realized outcomes. With the learned policy and predictive information held fixed, the validation-selected interior point `eta=0.5` improves held-out executed-path net Sharpe relative to the immediate-execution baseline `eta=1.0` by `+0.0105` at `kappa=5e-4` and `+0.0213` at `kappa=1e-3`, while reducing median executed turnover from `0.02200` to `0.01095`. The `kappa=0` row remains near-flat at `-0.0002`. This makes the selected-point comparison the only main empirical result in the workshop version. It is therefore best read as an implementation-side result that appears under frictions rather than as evidence of a stronger predictor.

Figure 1 then explains why executed-path evaluation is primary. Target-level quantities are diagnostic, not headline evaluation objects. At the validation-selected operating point `eta=0.5`, target turnover is about twice realized executed turnover across the locked cost rows (`TOtgt/TOexec ≈ 2.00`), tracking remains small but nonzero at about `0.00259`, and the final path gap grows with cost from `0.00067` at `kappa=0` to `0.00739` at `kappa=1e-3`. The accounting point is not that target-path numbers are uninformative; it is that they answer a different question. Once frictions intervene between proposed targets and realized positions, the system should be judged by the realized path it actually induces. The accounting diagnostic therefore serves as a mechanism explanation for the main result rather than as a competing result of its own.

If space allows, one compact supporting table can remain in the main text to make the forecasting relevance explicit. Under the conservative title `Similar forecasting information, different realized decision quality`, Table 2 compares `c=0` and the validation-selected `c=3000` while keeping the fitted ridge forecast map fixed and computing forecast metrics before the decision layer. The contrast is the key point: at `kappa=5e-4`, forecast MSE changes by only `-0.03%`, sign accuracy by `+0.41` percentage points, and rank IC by `+0.0038`, while executed turnover and realized cost both fall by `90.8%` and net Sharpe rises by `+0.9539`. At `kappa=1e-3`, forecast MSE changes by only `+0.28%`, sign accuracy by `+0.49` percentage points, and rank IC by `+0.0033`, while executed turnover and realized cost both fall by `95.1%` and net Sharpe rises by `+2.0208`. This supports the workshop-facing message that forecast quality and realized decision quality can diverge once a forecast-to-decision interface is involved. It remains supporting analysis only, and it does not justify an exact-same-forecast claim because previous executed weights remain part of the state.

Additional evidence should be pointed to briefly rather than promoted into the core story of a 4-page paper. Appendix materials include the dense-friction package, which is archived as a regenerated canonical diagnostic bundle under the locked protocol, and the CC-TA-LBIP comparator, which remains auxiliary supporting evidence. Both move in the same qualitative direction as the main result, but neither should displace the selected-point RL comparison as the primary empirical finding. For the workshop version, the paper is strongest when it makes one point clearly: with predictive information held fixed, changing the forecast-to-execution interface changes realized decision quality, and target-path quantities are useful for diagnosis but not for headline evaluation.

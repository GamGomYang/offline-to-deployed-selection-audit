This note records a **narrow auxiliary robustness check**, not a new main experiment. It is built from the existing compact CC-TA-LBIP artifacts only, without broad reruns, and keeps the same 918-dimensional state, the same fixed ridge forecast map, the same executed-path accounting, and the same validation-only selection rule for `c`.

The narrow held-out ablation over `c in {0, 2000, 3000, 4000}` on the main positive-cost regimes shows reviewer-safe neighborhood robustness around the documented operating point. At `kappa=5e-4`, net Sharpe rises from `0.0661` at `c=0` to `0.9328` at `c=2000`, `1.0199` at `c=3000`, and `1.0314` at `c=4000`, while executed turnover falls from `1.29032` to `0.16936`, `0.11894`, and `0.09178`. At `kappa=1e-3`, net Sharpe rises from `-1.0529` at `c=0` to `0.9549`, `0.9679`, and `0.9709`, while executed turnover falls from `1.29032` to `0.09178`, `0.06333`, and `0.04843`.

This ablation should be read as a **local robustness check for an auxiliary comparator**, not as post-hoc retuning and not as a second identification result. The archived operating point remains `c=3000` because the locked validation rule selects the smallest positive `c` within 95% of the best positive-cost validation score. Under that rule, `c=3000` is selected, `c=4000` qualifies but is not selected, and `c=2000` falls below the validation threshold. The held-out neighborhood therefore looks stable rather than knife-edge, but the selection logic remains unchanged.

The `kappa=0` collapse logic also remains intact in the underlying held-out aggregate even though the narrow table focuses on positive-cost rows: all displayed `c` arms are identical at `kappa=0`, with executed-path net Sharpe `1.18745` and executed turnover `1.29032`, so there is still no free zero-cost smoothing.

Reviewer-facing interpretation should stay conservative. Because `c` changes the turnover-aware decision layer while the ridge forecast map stays fixed, the comparator supports the message that cost-aware decision translation matters under frictions under the same-state, fixed-forecast setup. It does **not** prove a unique best `c`, it does **not** separate every regularization mechanism inside the optimizer from the broader interface effect, and it does **not** rise above auxiliary supporting evidence. Under those guardrails, this auxiliary package is strengthened in a reviewer-safe way without widening the paper's claim.

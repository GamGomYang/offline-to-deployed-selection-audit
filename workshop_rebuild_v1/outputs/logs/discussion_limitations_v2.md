The discussion should repeat one narrow conclusion: in this case study, primary evaluation should follow realized executed decisions rather than counterfactual targets, and forecast-to-execution interfaces matter under costs because they change realized turnover, realized cost, and realized decision quality. The gain should continue to be described as implementation-side, not as evidence of a stronger predictive signal.

The limitations should stay explicit and specific. This is a portfolio-domain case study rather than a general theorem about forecasting systems. The empirical setting is a fixed 27-name snapshot under the canonical frozen split rather than a broad universe study. The central identification result is a frozen-policy comparison, not an end-to-end retraining study. The dense-friction figure in this rebuild lane is a regenerated canonical diagnostic bundle under the locked protocol, not a recovered source-artifact reproduction. The CC-TA-LBIP comparator remains auxiliary supporting evidence rather than a second main identification result, and its narrow `c`-ablation is only a local robustness check around the selected operating point, not proof of a uniquely optimal `c`. The same-forecast table remains conservative because exact forecast identity across arms has not been directly established. The paper does not claim passive benchmark dominance, production readiness, or broad universality.

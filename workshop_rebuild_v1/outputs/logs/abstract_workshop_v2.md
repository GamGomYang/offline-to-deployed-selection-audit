Title: Forecast-to-Execution Interfaces Matter in Cost-Sensitive Portfolio Decisions

Forecasting-driven decision systems are often evaluated as if proposed targets were immediately realized. Under transaction costs, this can conflate proposed targets with realized positions and misstate realized decision quality. We study a forecast-to-execution interface that separates target and executed portfolios and attaches primary evaluation to the realized path in a frozen-policy portfolio decision setting. Under a frozen learned policy, the validation-selected interior operating point `eta=0.5` improves executed-path net Sharpe in positive-cost regimes while roughly halving executed turnover, with negligible zero-cost effect. Accounting diagnostics, a regenerated canonical dense-friction diagnostic bundle under the locked protocol, and auxiliary evidence from a linear forecast-plus-convex-optimization comparator all support an implementation-side interpretation without upgrading the claim beyond this narrow case study.
